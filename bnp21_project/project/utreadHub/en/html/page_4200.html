Standards
Objectives of Standardization

The development of standard XML documents, according to the standardized document creation rules, aims to provide openness and interoperability for standard e-commerce infrastructure and therefore promote e-Trade.

Expected Benefits
  • · Maintenance of consistency in electronic documents through standardized document formats and development process.
  • · Achievement of interoperability in e-Trade
  • · Create the foundation for a universal e-commerce network through the adoption of global
Adoption of uTradeHub Document Standards
Domestic Standards
  • · Guidelines to the development of XML Electronic documents (v3.0), Korea Institute for Electronic Commerce (KIEC)
  • · Guidelines to Routing , KIEC
  • · KIEC XML CC Library, KIEC
International Standards
  • · Core Component Technical Specification, UN/CEFACT TMG, 2003
  • · XML Naming & Design Rules, UN/CEFACT ATG, 2004
  • · UN/CEFACT Modeling Methodology, UN/CEFACT TMG, 2002
Chronology of Standardization Efforts in Korea
  • · Sep. 2005: Selected candidate documents for Standardization
  • · Jan. ~ Dec. 2006: Standardization of Documents
  • · Jan. ~ Mar. 2007: Preparation for Registration of Domestic Standard
  • · Apr. ~ Nov. 2007: Registration of Domestic Standard (a total of 102 electronic documents)
    • - Electronic documents related to international finance (57), 25 relating to international trade, six relating to land transportation, six relating to customs clearance and 8 relating to insurance.
  • · Jan. 2008 : Registered as the Domestic Standard by the Notification No. -01/2008of the Ministry of Knowledge Economy
Activities through UN/CEFACT (United Nations Center for Trade Facilitation and Electronic Business)

The case of uTradeHub development as a single window for trade related activities was presented at the working group meeting for UN/CEFACT. Further analysis of the technological trends and application of the system was also introduced.

Key Working Group Activities
  • · UneDocs: Development of universal electronic trade document standards (TBG2)
  • · International Trade Facilitation: Development of recommendations on trade facilitation (TBG15)
  • · Applied Technologies Group: Aiming to develop technologies to create and maintain XML-based electronic document standard XML (ATG2)
